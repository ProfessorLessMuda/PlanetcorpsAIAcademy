# Lean AI Sigma -- Green Belt: Learning Path

## Overview

This learning path follows the PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) cycle, the same framework you will use to improve prompt efficiency in practice. Each phase builds on the previous one, and assessments validate readiness before advancing.

**Estimated time to completion:** 8-12 hours of focused study and practice.

---

## Phase 1: Plan -- Foundations

**Goal:** Understand the principles and vocabulary of Lean AI before writing your first optimized prompt.

### Study
1. Read the [Green Belt Certification Guide](exam-guide-summary.md) -- understand the exam format, domain weights, and certification requirements
2. Read [Domains & Task Statements](domains.md) -- scan all 25 task statements to understand the full scope
3. Skim the [Key Concepts Glossary](key-concepts-glossary.md) -- familiarize yourself with terminology
4. **Study Module 01:** Prompt Waste & Lean AI Principles -- learn the 8 waste types, Lean principles, and HALO concept
5. **Study Module 02:** CORPS Framework & Prompt Design -- master all 5 CORPS elements

### Practice
- Identify waste types in 3-5 prompts from your daily work (no optimization yet -- just diagnosis)

### Assess
- Take the Domain 1 Assessment (target: 70%+)
- Take the Domain 2 Assessment (target: 70%+)

### Milestone
You can identify all 8 prompt waste types by name, explain the 5 CORPS elements, and apply CORPS to construct a complete prompt from scratch.

---

## Phase 2: Do -- Applied Practice

**Goal:** Apply CORPS and context engineering to real tasks. Begin measuring results.

### Study
6. **Study Module 03:** Context Engineering -- learn context vs. prompt engineering, JIT delivery, context hierarchies
7. **Study Module 04:** Token Efficiency & Optimization -- master compression, output optimization, first-pass precision

### Practice
- Complete [Exercise 1: CORPS Before/After Prompt Redesign](hands-on-exercises.md) -- achieve 30%+ token reduction on a real task
- Complete [Exercise 2: Token Reduction Challenge](hands-on-exercises.md) -- find the minimum viable prompt through progressive reduction

### Assess
- Take the Domain 3 Assessment (target: 70%+)
- Take the Domain 4 Assessment (target: 70%+)

### Milestone
You can reduce token usage by 30%+ on a real prompt using CORPS and context engineering. You can distinguish context engineering from prompt engineering and apply JIT context delivery.

---

## Phase 3: Check -- Measurement & Analysis

**Goal:** Master quantitative measurement of prompt efficiency and understand the broader implications of token waste.

### Study
8. **Study Module 05:** Measurement, Metrics & Sustainability -- LAI scoring, 4 key metrics, PDCA, energy awareness

### Practice
- Complete [Exercise 3: LAI Score Calculation Lab](hands-on-exercises.md) -- calculate and interpret LAI scores
- Complete [Exercise 4: Energy Reflection & PDCA Journal](hands-on-exercises.md) -- track real usage, estimate energy, apply PDCA

### Assess
- Take the Domain 5 Assessment (target: 70%+)

### Milestone
You can calculate LAI scores, track all 4 efficiency metrics, execute a PDCA cycle, and articulate the energy cost of token waste.

---

## Phase 4: Act -- Certification Readiness

**Goal:** Integrate all skills, practice under exam conditions, and assemble the certification portfolio.

### Review
- Revisit any domain where your assessment score was below 80%
- Re-read the glossary entries for terms you struggled with
- Review the [Cross-Reference Map](cross-reference-map.md) to understand how concepts connect across domains

### Practice
- Complete all Exam Scenarios (multi-domain integrated questions)
- Take a timed mock exam (35 domain questions + 12 scenario questions in 60 minutes)

### Portfolio Assembly
Compile the four certification deliverables:
1. Before/After Prompt Comparison (from Exercise 1)
2. Token Usage Analysis (from Exercise 4 session log)
3. Efficiency Improvement Report (from Exercise 3 and Exercise 4 PDCA)
4. Energy and Grid Impact Reflection (from Exercise 4)

### Milestone
You consistently score 80%+ on timed assessments and your portfolio demonstrates practical competence across all 5 domains.

---

## Tips for Success

- **Think before prompting.** This is the core principle. Resist the urge to start typing immediately.
- **Measure everything.** Intuition is useful but metrics are authoritative. Track your tokens and iterations.
- **Practice on real tasks.** The exercises use your actual work, not hypothetical scenarios. The skills transfer directly.
- **Review wrong answers carefully.** Each wrong assessment answer reveals a gap in understanding. Trace it to the specific task statement and re-read that section.
- **Use PDCA on your own learning.** Plan what to study, do the studying, check your assessment results, act on the gaps.
