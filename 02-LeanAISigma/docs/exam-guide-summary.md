# Lean AI Sigma -- Green Belt: Certification Guide

## Certification Overview

**Certification Name:** Lean AI Sigma -- Green Belt
**Purpose:** Validates that practitioners can design efficient, waste-free AI interactions using the CORPS framework, context engineering, and Lean principles.
**Tagline:** "From trial-and-error prompting to precision-engineered AI interactions."

---

## Exam Format

**Question Types:**
- Multiple choice (A, B, C, D) -- select the best answer
- Scenario-based questions -- read a situation, apply principles to choose the correct approach

**Question Distribution:**

| Domain | Name | Weight | Domain Questions | Scenario Questions |
|--------|------|--------|------------------|--------------------|
| 1 | Prompt Waste & Lean AI Principles | 15% | 6 | Included in scenarios |
| 2 | CORPS Framework & Prompt Design | 25% | 8 | Included in scenarios |
| 3 | Context Engineering | 20% | 7 | Included in scenarios |
| 4 | Token Efficiency & Optimization | 20% | 7 | Included in scenarios |
| 5 | Measurement, Metrics & Sustainability | 20% | 7 | Included in scenarios |
| **Total** | | **100%** | **35** | **12** |

**Exam Scenarios:** 4 integrated scenarios testing across multiple domains, 3 questions each (12 scenario questions total).

**Passing Score:** 70% per domain, 75% overall.

**Time Limit:** 60 minutes for the full exam (domain questions + scenarios).

---

## Certification Requirements

To earn the Green Belt, candidates must:

1. **Pass the exam** -- Meet the scoring thresholds above
2. **Submit a certification portfolio** with four deliverables:
   - Before/After Prompt Comparison (30%+ token reduction demonstrated)
   - Token Usage Analysis (10+ interactions measured)
   - Efficiency Improvement Report (with LAI scores and trend data)
   - Energy and Grid Impact Reflection (250-500 words)

## Domain Summaries

### Domain 1: Prompt Waste & Lean AI Principles (15%)
Covers the 8 types of prompt waste (mapped from Lean manufacturing), value stream mapping for AI interactions, pull-based design, JIT context, Kaizen, and the HALO concept. Questions test waste identification in prompt examples and Lean principle application.

### Domain 2: CORPS Framework & Prompt Design (25%)
The highest-weighted domain. Covers all 5 CORPS elements (Context, Objective, Role, Parameters, Structure) in depth, including when to use or skip each element, integration techniques, and before/after optimization. Questions test element application, missing-element diagnosis, and full integration.

### Domain 3: Context Engineering (20%)
Covers the distinction between context and prompt engineering, JIT context delivery, context window management (token budgeting, "lost in the middle"), context hierarchies (persistent/situational/ephemeral), and context relevance assessment (signal-to-noise ratio, A/B testing, pruning).

### Domain 4: Token Efficiency & Optimization (20%)
Covers token measurement and baselines, compression techniques (redundancy elimination, concise phrasing, role-based compression), output optimization (length constraints, format constraints), first-pass precision, and the "Name That Tune" challenge methodology.

### Domain 5: Measurement, Metrics & Sustainability (20%)
Covers the LAI Score formula and calculation, the 4 key metrics (tokens per output, iterations, time, improvement %), PDCA cycle application, energy/environmental impact of token waste, and certification deliverable design.

## Study Recommendations

1. **Start with Domains 1 and 2.** They provide the foundational vocabulary and the core framework. Everything else builds on them.
2. **Spend the most time on Domain 2.** It carries 25% of the exam weight and is the most practice-intensive.
3. **Use the exercises.** The hands-on exercises (especially Exercise 1 and Exercise 2) build the practical intuition that assessment questions test.
4. **Track your own metrics.** Apply what you learn to your daily AI usage. The best exam preparation is real-world practice.
5. **Review wrong answers.** Each incorrect assessment answer points to a specific task statement. Re-read that section of the curriculum module.

---

## Exam Strategy

- **Read scenarios carefully.** Scenario questions test judgment, not recall. The correct answer is often the one that addresses the root cause, not the symptom.
- **Watch for "always" and "never."** Absolutes are usually wrong. Lean AI is about tradeoffs and context-appropriate decisions.
- **When in doubt, think waste.** Many questions can be resolved by identifying which option eliminates the most waste or addresses the root waste type.
- **CORPS is the Swiss Army knife.** If a question asks how to improve a prompt, the answer almost always involves applying or completing the CORPS framework.
- **Measurement beats intuition.** If one answer proposes measuring and another proposes guessing, measurement is almost always correct.
