# Learning Path

A four-phase study plan for the FiftyOne: Visual AI Data Quality for Everyone training. Each phase builds on the previous one. Estimated total time: 12-20 hours depending on depth.

---

## Phase 1: Foundations (3-4 hours)

**Goal:** Understand what computer vision is, why data quality matters, and what FiftyOne does.

### Checklist
- [ ] Read Training Guide Overview
- [ ] Read Domains & Learning Objectives
- [ ] Skim Key Concepts Glossary
- [ ] Study Module 01: Computer Vision Fundamentals
- [ ] Study Module 02: FiftyOne Platform & Architecture
- [ ] Take Domain 1 Assessment
- [ ] Take Domain 2 Assessment

### Milestone
You can explain what computer vision is, why data quality matters more than model architecture for most AI projects, and what FiftyOne does — to any audience.

### Audience Notes
- **Business leaders:** This phase gives you everything you need. You can stop here or continue to Phase 2 Module 4 for the data exploration deep dive.
- **MBA/MSBA students:** Complete this phase fully, then proceed to Phase 2.
- **Technical learners:** Move through quickly — the concepts will be familiar. Focus on the FiftyOne-specific content in Module 2.

---

## Phase 2: Hands-On Skills (4-6 hours)

**Goal:** Load data into FiftyOne, explore it visually, and find quality issues.

### Checklist
- [ ] Study Module 03: Data Loading & Management
- [ ] Study Module 04: Visual Data Exploration & Quality
- [ ] Complete Exercise 1: FiftyOne Quickstart
- [ ] Complete Exercise 2: Explore and Audit a Dataset
- [ ] Take Domain 3 Assessment
- [ ] Take Domain 4 Assessment

### Milestone
You can load a dataset into FiftyOne, apply filters and ViewStages to explore it, identify class imbalances and label errors, and use FiftyOne Brain for embedding-based analysis.

### Audience Notes
- **Data analysts:** This is your core phase. Focus on Module 4 — it covers the exploration and quality analysis skills you'll use daily.
- **MBA/MSBA students:** Complete Modules 3-4 and Exercise 1. Exercise 2 is optional but recommended.
- **Technical builders:** Complete everything including both exercises. You'll build on these skills in Phase 3.

---

## Phase 3: Model Evaluation & Workflows (3-5 hours)

**Goal:** Evaluate model predictions, compare models, and design data improvement workflows.

### Checklist
- [ ] Study Module 05: Model Evaluation & AI Workflows
- [ ] Complete Exercise 3: Evaluate Model Predictions
- [ ] Complete Exercise 4: End-to-End Data Quality Workflow
- [ ] Take Domain 5 Assessment

### Milestone
You can evaluate model performance using FiftyOne's API, find systematic failure patterns, compare multiple models, and design an iterative data quality improvement workflow.

### Audience Notes
- **Data analysts:** Focus on the evaluation concepts and Exercise 3. Exercise 4 is the capstone.
- **Technical builders:** This is your critical phase. Complete everything — Exercise 4 is the closest thing to real-world workflow design.
- **MBA/MSBA students:** Read Module 5 for concepts. Skip the exercises unless you want hands-on practice.

---

## Phase 4: Mastery & Application (2-5 hours)

**Goal:** Apply everything to realistic scenarios and demonstrate comprehensive understanding.

### Checklist
- [ ] Complete All Exam Scenarios
- [ ] Take Timed Assessment (all domains, 45-minute time limit)
- [ ] Review Weak Domains
- [ ] Score 80%+ on Scenario Questions

### Milestone
You can consistently score 80%+ on timed assessments and explain FiftyOne's value to any audience — from a business executive to a machine learning engineer.

### Audience Notes
- **All audiences:** The scenario assessments are designed to test judgment, not code knowledge. Every audience should complete them.
- **Technical builders:** Aim for 90%+ and review any domain where you score below 80%.

## Tips for Success

1. **Don't skip the exercises.** Reading about FiftyOne is useful; using it is transformative. Even 30 minutes of hands-on exploration builds more understanding than hours of reading.

2. **Read the assessment explanations.** Wrong answers are learning opportunities. The explanations describe why incorrect options fail — these edge cases are where real understanding lives.

3. **Connect to your own data.** After completing Exercise 2 with the Zoo dataset, try loading your own images into FiftyOne. The concepts become concrete when you see them in your own data.

4. **Take notes on the Lean AI Sigma connections.** Each module ties FiftyOne concepts to process improvement principles. These connections are what separate someone who can use a tool from someone who can improve a process.
